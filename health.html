Allopathy

Allopathy is the modern, science-based medicine system used worldwide. It includes pharmaceuticals, surgeries, and fast-acting treatments for acute and chronic illnesses. Commonly used in hospitals and clinics, allopathy is evidence-based and widely trusted.

Ayurveda

Ayurveda is an ancient Indian system of medicine based on natural healing using herbs, diet, and lifestyle practices. It focuses on balancing body energies (Doshas) for long-term wellness. Herbal remedies, detox, yoga, and massages are key elements of this system.

Naturopathy

Naturopathy promotes natural healing by encouraging lifestyle changes, healthy nutrition, exercise, and stress management. It avoids drugs and surgeries, instead relying on techniques like fasting, hydrotherapy, massage, and meditation to heal the body naturally.

Homeopathy

Homeopathy is a gentle form of treatment based on the principle of “like cures like.” It uses highly diluted substances to stimulate the body’s self-healing response. It is widely used for allergies, migraines, depression, skin disorders, and chronic diseases.

Antibiotics

Antibiotics are drugs that kill or prevent the growth of bacteria. They are used to treat infections such as pneumonia, tuberculosis, and urinary tract infections. While effective, their overuse can lead to antibiotic resistance, so proper use is crucial.
